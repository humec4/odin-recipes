Perogies

Description

Perogies taste much better when made from scratch. These Polish perogies use sour cream in the dough for added flavour. Make a big batch and throw them in the freezer for quick and delicious meals down the road. Enjoy!

Ingredients

Dough:
  • 4 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp. salt
  • 2 cups sour cream
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 tbsp. melted butter
  • 2 tbsp. vegetable oil
Filling:
  • 8 baking potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Stir flour and salt together in large bowl.
  2. Whisk sour cream, eggs, egg yolk, butter and oil together in a separate bowl.
  3. Stir wet ingredients into dry until well blended. Cover bowl with a towel and let stand 15-20 mins.
  4. Place potatoes into a pot and fill with enough water to cover. Bring to a boil and cool until tender, about 15 mins.
  5. Drain water from potatoes and mash with shredded cheese. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside to cool.
  6. Separate perogie dough into two balls. Roll out one ball at a time on lightly floured surface.
  7. Cut into circles using a cookie cutter or glass, 3 to 4 inches in diameter.
  8. Assemble perogies. Brush a little water around the edges of the circle and spoon some filling into the center.
  9. Fold the circle in half and press to seal the edges (or use a fork for crimped edges).
  10. Place perogies on a cookie sheet and freeze. Once frozen transfer to freezer bags.
  11. To cook your pierogies you can boil or fry them. Place perogies in boiling water and boil until they float to the top. Or fry on medium-low heat until they are no longer frozen in the center.
